Random Task Generator

One task a day, to reconnect with your imagination!

Topic

Working with ollama turned out to be interesting in many ways. Ollama is an AI-powered tool used to generate text and content. It uses advanced language models to automatically generate content based on given prompts. In preparation for working with the tool, we had a discussion within the class on the topic of opportunities and risks of AI, which further illustrated to me the value and importance of dealing with the topic of AI. This type of AI tool can be used in a wide variety of areas. We use it primarily to prototype our project ideas and to learn more efficient “prompt engineering”. The project made it possible to develop completely new ideas for projects that arose through the use of AI.

Idea

As I had previously watched a documentary about our increasing need for self-optimization and the desire to constantly be productive in our society, I decided that it could be interesting to turn the whole concept of "to-dos" around and thus to create a generative task assigner that would give you one completely useless and totally random task a day. By building a prompt that would allow the tool to create one task a day that was manageable in the near environment, the goal was to inspire the user to return to a childlike, imaginative state, in which things do not always have to have a deeper sense in order to be important to us.

Process

I started out with prompt engineering and learning how to work with Ollama. It was sometimes quite experimental, as AI is mostly a black box, and you don't always know exactly what is happening inside. The AI could recognize certain special characters, and giving out examples also worked quite well. Fabian explained that it helps to really just experiment with which words and phrases would work particularly well. I ended up with quite a long prompt, intending for a random task that was supposed to be childlike, creative, and also realistic (set in the user's close environment and able to be successfully completed within up to 5 minutes). Adding examples also turned out to be very useful. Furthermore, I added a button where the user could choose their mood of the day, which would then be included in the prompt. The task would match or reflect the mood of the day. This could make the whole concept slightly more interactive. The quality of the results vary, but overall I am very happy with the result.

Takeaways

I was glad to have had the opportunity to create something so absurd and to have the space and capacity to realize a project like this. It was a motivation to see how diverse and experimental projects with AI can look like. This being said, I now recognize how AI can be used as a new approach for creativity and not just a threat. I felt that it had improved my understanding of working with AI. Our conversation about the risks and potentials of AI was particularly interesting to me, as the topic is of high importance for our current society and also for our futures working in the creative field. Though there of course are many files and areas in which the usage of AI could have a very positive impact, the risks and projects that are being developed right now, were very shocking to me as I prepared for the discussion. Especially the input on our data security and how our images could potentially be used as AI training data, was very distressing for me. I very much valued Fabians impact on the discussion, and it gave me a new sense as to why what we do has an important value for us, even if we don't feel like working in the coding scene later on. I would have enjoyed the opportunity for this discussion much earlier on, because it gives important information about the political importance of knowing how the web is build.
